# Step 1 — Reorganize Financial Statements (McKinsey Valuation, 4th ed., Ch 7)

> Pure 4th-edition book methodology, extracted via NotebookLM. Modern refinements live in `modern-updates.md`.

## Purpose
Standard financial statements commingle operating performance, nonoperating items, and capital structure. This commingling biases traditional measures such as Return on Assets (ROA) and Return on Equity (ROE). We reorganize the balance sheet and income statement into **operating vs. nonoperating** categories so we can compute three measures that are independent of leverage and focus solely on the core business's ability to create value:

- **NOPLAT** — after-tax operating profit available to all investors
- **Invested capital** — investor capital required to fund core operations
- **Free cash flow (FCF)** — after-tax cash from core operations after reinvestment

These let an analyst evaluate the company's true economic performance, independent of its capital structure and nonoperating activities.

## Definitions & formulas

### NOPLAT
**NOPLAT (Net Operating Profit Less Adjusted Taxes)** is the total after-tax operating income generated by the company's invested capital that is **available to all financial investors** (debt and equity holders).

**(a) Operating build-up (from EBITA):**
```
  Net sales
- Cost of merchandise sold
- Selling, general and administrative expenses
- Depreciation
= Operating profit (EBITA)        [Earnings before interest, taxes, and amortization of goodwill]
+ Operating lease interest         [add-back to treat leases as debt]
= Adjusted EBITA
- Operating cash taxes on EBITA
= NOPLAT
```

**(b) Financing-method reconciliation (NOPLAT tied to net income):**
```
  Net earnings (net income)
+ Increase in deferred taxes
+ Goodwill amortization
= Adjusted net income
+ After-tax interest expense
+ After-tax lease interest expense
+ Loss (gain) from discontinued operations
= Total income available to investors
- After-tax interest income
= NOPLAT
```

**Operating taxes on EBITA** are the hypothetical taxes the company would pay if it were financed entirely with equity. To eliminate the tax effects of each nonoperating item, **multiply each line item's dollar amount by the company's marginal tax rate**.
```
  Provision for income taxes (reported taxes)
- Taxes on interest income
+ Tax shield on interest expense
+ Tax shield on operating lease interest expense
= Operating taxes on EBITA
```

**Convert to operating cash taxes** (the book recommends using cash taxes actually paid) by adjusting for deferred taxes. An increase in deferred tax liabilities is a reported-but-not-paid expense and an equity equivalent, so it is subtracted from operating taxes to get to cash taxes:
```
  Operating taxes on EBITA
- (Increase) in deferred taxes        [decrease adds back]
= Operating cash taxes on EBITA
```

**Equivalent formulas the book uses:**
- Reorganization context: `NOPLAT = EBITA − Operating taxes on EBITA + Increase in deferred taxes`
  (subtracting "operating cash taxes on EBITA" is mathematically equivalent to subtracting operating taxes and adding back the deferred-tax increase).
- Key-value-driver / ROIC decomposition context: `NOPLAT = EBITA × (1 − operating cash tax rate)`.

**Marginal vs. effective tax rate guidance:** Use the **marginal tax rate** — "the tax rate on an extra dollar of income," generally the sum of federal and state income taxes — for the operating-tax adjustments. "Marginal taxes do not equal average taxes, which are computed by dividing reported taxes by earnings before taxes." The average (effective) rate can vary dramatically with one-time items, whereas the marginal rate is relatively constant.

### Invested capital
**Invested capital** is the cumulative amount of investor capital required to fund core operations, without distinction as to how that capital is financed. It equals **operating assets minus operating liabilities**.

**Build-up stack:**
```
  Operating working capital
+ Net property and equipment (net PP&E)
+ Capitalized operating leases
+ Net other assets (other operating assets − other operating liabilities)
= Invested capital (excluding goodwill and acquired intangibles)
+ Acquired intangibles and goodwill
+ Cumulative amortization and pooled goodwill        [recapture historical write-offs]
= Invested capital (including goodwill)
+ Nonoperating assets (excess cash, marketable securities, nonconsolidated subsidiaries, other long-term investments)
= Total funds invested
```

**Operating working capital** = operating current assets − (non-interest-bearing) operating current liabilities.

Operating current assets (in order):
- Working cash (estimated as roughly **2 percent of sales**)
- Receivables, net
- Merchandise inventories
- Other current assets (e.g., prepaid expenses)
- *Excluded:* excess cash and marketable securities

Operating current liabilities (in order):
- Accounts payable
- Accrued salaries (accrued compensation)
- Deferred revenue
- Other accrued expenses (including income taxes payable)
- *Excluded:* short-term interest-bearing debt, notes payable, dividends payable (financing items)

**Invested capital excluding goodwill** measures internal operating performance. **Invested capital including goodwill** measures performance for shareholders by including premiums paid for acquisitions. Reported goodwill must be adjusted **upward** to recapture historical amortization and impairments, because goodwill does not "wear out" economically.

**Total funds invested identity:**
```
Invested capital + Nonoperating assets = Debt & equivalents + Equity & equivalents = Total funds invested
```
- **Debt equivalents:** unfunded retirement liabilities (pensions / postretirement medical), capitalized operating leases, restructuring provisions (if treated as nonoperating).
- **Equity equivalents:** deferred income taxes (net deferred taxes), cumulative amortization / pooled goodwill (offsetting the asset adjustment), income-smoothing provisions.

### Free cash flow
**Free cash flow** is the after-tax cash flow generated by core operations after deducting investments in new capital. It is independent of nonoperating items and capital structure.

**Exact formula / build-up:**
```
  NOPLAT
+ Depreciation (and noncash operating charges, e.g., employee stock options)
= Gross cash flow
- Increase in operating working capital
- Net capital expenditures             [= change in net PP&E + depreciation]
- Investment in capitalized operating leases (change in capitalized value)
- Investment in goodwill and acquired intangibles (change in net intangibles + amortization)
- Increase in net other operating assets
= Free cash flow
```

The book labels the sum of those deductions **"gross investment."** Because depreciation is added to NOPLAT to reach gross cash flow and then added back into the change in net PP&E to define net capital expenditures, **depreciation flows through and cancels out**, making the total subtraction equivalent to the **net investment** in capital.

FCF is distinct from **cash flow to investors / financing flows**: FCF measures cash thrown off by operations before financing, whereas cash flow to investors is how that cash (plus nonoperating flows) is distributed to and from debt and equity holders.

## Step-by-step procedure
1. **Classify every balance-sheet line** as operating, nonoperating, or financing. Pull excess cash, marketable securities, and nonconsolidated subsidiaries out as nonoperating assets; pull debt, notes payable, and dividends payable out as financing.
2. **Build operating working capital:** sum operating current assets (working cash ≈ 2% of sales, net receivables, inventories, other current assets) and subtract operating current liabilities (accounts payable, accrued salaries, deferred revenue, other accrued expenses incl. income taxes payable).
3. **Add net PP&E, capitalized operating leases, and net other operating assets** to get invested capital excluding goodwill. Add adjusted goodwill and acquired intangibles (recapturing cumulative amortization / pooled goodwill) to get invested capital including goodwill. Add nonoperating assets to get **total funds invested**; confirm it equals debt & equivalents + equity & equivalents.
4. **Compute EBITA** from the income statement (operating profit before interest, taxes, and goodwill amortization). Add back operating lease interest to get Adjusted EBITA.
5. **Compute operating taxes on EBITA:** start from reported provision for income taxes, subtract taxes on interest income, add tax shields on interest expense and on operating lease interest expense (each at the marginal tax rate).
6. **Convert to operating cash taxes** by subtracting the increase in deferred taxes (add back a decrease). Subtract operating cash taxes from Adjusted EBITA to get **NOPLAT**.
7. **Build FCF:** NOPLAT + depreciation/noncash charges = gross cash flow; subtract the increase in operating working capital, net capital expenditures, investment in capitalized operating leases, investment in goodwill & acquired intangibles, and increase in net other operating assets = **free cash flow**.
8. **Reconcile** (below) to verify the reorganization is complete.

## Reconciliation check
FCF must tie to the financing side ("total financing flow"). Reconcile the **origin** of cash to its **disposition**:

**Cash flow available to investors (origin):**
```
  Free cash flow
+ After-tax interest income (from excess cash)
+ Decrease (increase) in excess cash
+ Decrease (increase) in nonoperating assets (e.g., nonconsolidated subsidiaries)
+ Loss (gain) from discontinued operations
```

**Financing flow (disposition):**
```
  After-tax interest expense
+ After-tax lease interest expense
+ Decrease (increase) in debt
+ Decrease (increase) in capitalized operating leases
+ Dividends paid
+ Net shares repurchased (issued)
+ Decrease (increase) in debt equivalents (retirement liabilities, restructuring provisions)
+ Decrease (increase) in dividends payable
```
The two totals must equal. The book also offers a second internal check: NOPLAT computed via the operating method (from EBITA) must equal NOPLAT via the financing method (reconciled from net income).

## Common pitfalls (from the book)
- **Using ROA / ROE on unadjusted statements** — they commingle operating, nonoperating, and capital-structure effects and bias the analysis.
- **Using the average (effective) tax rate instead of the marginal rate** for the operating-tax adjustments — the average rate swings with one-time items; use the marginal rate.
- **Forgetting deferred taxes** — NOPLAT should be on a cash-tax basis; subtract the increase in deferred tax liabilities (an equity equivalent) from operating taxes on EBITA.
- **Leaving excess cash / marketable securities in invested capital** — they are nonoperating and must be excluded; only working cash (~2% of sales) is operating.
- **Including short-term debt, notes payable, or dividends payable in operating working capital** — these are financing items and must be excluded.
- **Not recapturing goodwill write-offs** — reported goodwill must be adjusted upward for cumulative amortization and impairments because goodwill does not economically wear out; otherwise invested capital is understated.
- **Mixing nonoperating income into NOPLAT** — income, gains, or losses from excess cash, nonconsolidated subsidiaries, and discontinued operations are excluded from NOPLAT and valued separately.
- **Mishandling operating leases** — capitalize them as both an operating asset and a debt equivalent; add the implied lease interest back to EBITA and include the change in capitalized value in gross investment.

## Inputs needed / Outputs produced
- **Inputs (raw line items):** net sales; cost of merchandise sold; SG&A; depreciation; goodwill amortization; interest expense; interest income; provision for income taxes; deferred taxes (balance and change); operating-lease detail (rentals / implied interest, capitalized value); cash and marketable securities (split working vs. excess); receivables; inventories; other current assets; accounts payable; accrued salaries; deferred revenue; other accrued expenses (incl. income taxes payable); net PP&E; other long-term operating assets and liabilities; goodwill and acquired intangibles (incl. cumulative amortization / pooled goodwill); nonoperating assets (nonconsolidated subsidiaries, other investments); debt and notes payable; retirement / restructuring provisions; equity; dividends paid; share repurchases/issuances; discontinued operations gain/loss; marginal tax rate.
- **Outputs:** NOPLAT; invested capital excluding goodwill; invested capital including goodwill; free cash flow; total funds invested (reconciled to debt & equivalents + equity & equivalents).
